MusoHQ Privacy Policy

Last updated: 6 July 2026

MusoHQ is built to collect as little as possible. There is no account, no server, and no analytics. Here is exactly what that means.

What MusoHQ does not do

  • It does not require or create an account.
  • It does not send any data to a server — there is no server.
  • It does not use any analytics or advertising SDK.
  • It does not share, sell, or transmit any information to the developer or anyone else.

What is stored, and where

Everything MusoHQ remembers — your invoices, band members and payout splits, expenses, setlists, gig times, and employment income — is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. None of it leaves your device automatically.

Sharing invoices and exports

When you choose to send an invoice, share a band member's payout, or export a CSV summary, MusoHQ hands that file or text to Apple's standard share sheet. Where it goes from there — email, Messages, saving to Files — is entirely your choice at that moment. MusoHQ itself does not transmit it anywhere.

Purchases

The one-time MusoHQ Pro unlock is processed entirely by Apple through StoreKit. The developer never receives or has access to your payment details — that relationship is between you and Apple.

Children's privacy

MusoHQ is a business and tax record-keeping tool aimed at working musicians. It is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, of any age, since it does not collect information at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes — for example, if a future version of the app adds a feature that does collect data — this page will be updated and the date above will change.
